          Interactive analysis of AI data
          center impacts in Illinois




People v. AI
                                                     Introduction



This project evaluates environmental, social, and economic impacts of
accelerated AI data center development across Illinois using an
interactive research platform.


It operationalizes an Annoyance Threshold metric and delivers visual
tools for evidence‑based community engagement, permitting, and
policy decisions.
01
Project
Overview
                                                 Title & Abstract



The People v. Hasty AI Development maps existing, proposed, and
denied data centers to assess multi‑dimensional impacts.


The platform integrates spatial data, surveys, and county stress
indicators to enable interactive, policy‑relevant analysis.
Objectives &
Scope
Quantify site and county impacts and deﬁne
the Annoyance Threshold linking exposure to
harm.



Develop a reproducible, scalable framework
for permitting assessment and public
engagement across Illinois and beyond.
                            Stakeholders & Audience



Primary: local residents, municipal ofﬁcials, regulators, and utility
planners.


Secondary: researchers, NGOs, investors, and tech ﬁrms seeking
transparent impact assessment and mitigation pathways.
 Community Impacts




02
                             Environmental Concerns



Assess water consumption, heat emissions, land‑use change, and grid
load with spatial and temporal indicators.


Visualize environmental risk hotspots to guide siting, permitting, and
mitigation strategies.
                                            Social Well‑being



Measure noise, trafﬁc, housing pressure, and perceived nuisance via
surveys and administrative data.


Deﬁne the Annoyance Threshold as a composite index signaling
measurable declines in community quality of life.
      Economic Effects
Estimate direct jobs, construction spending,
and ongoing operations against potential tax
base shifts and local displacement risks.


Model ﬁscal trade‑offs to inform equitable
beneﬁt‑sharing and mitigation strategies for
impacted communities.
03
Methods &
Metrics
               Annoyance Threshold Concept



Deﬁne Annoyance Threshold as the exposure level where complaints
and health signals rise measurably.


Integrate surveys with objective metrics (noise, trafﬁc, heat) into a
composite index to guide siting and mitigation.
                                 Data Centers Inventory



Catalog existing, proposed, and denied Illinois sites with geolocation,
capacity, permitting status, and utility links.


Maintain lifecycle metadata to support scenario analysis and temporal
impact assessment.
County Stress
Indicators

Aggregate county metrics: grid load, water
stress, housing pressure, unemployment, and
public health signals.



Normalize and weight indicators to produce
a comparative county stress score for
vulnerability mapping.

Three county proﬁles illustrate outcomes: high impact, adaptive

Highlight drivers: utility capacity, governance, socioeconomic

             Global Infrastructure Relevance



Illinois trends mirror global patterns: siting near cheap power/water
and uneven community protections.


The framework is adaptable for cross‑regional comparisons and
international policy learning.
                                                     Conclusions



The platform links site inventories, Annoyance Thresholds, and county
stress scores into a reproducible assessment framework.


Recommend adaptive permitting, community beneﬁt agreements, and
dashboard transparency to balance AI infrastructure growth with local
resilience.
